About me:

I'm currently re-training as a Front-end Web Developer through an intensive course run by nology.

In my free time, I enjoy coding, making music, playing strategic board games and dogwalking.

More about me:

I grew up in St. Helens, an industrial town in the North West of England.

I studied Computer Science in Leicester, spent a couple of years in Oxford and then migrated to London where I worked as a freelance writer for a few magazines before getting a job as Production Editor on the Daily Telegraph's website. After 5 years at The Telegraph, I left and got a job in the public sector at CSCI (now CQC) the Commission for Social Care Inspection, where I worked with a Web Team specialising in Accessibility.

After dabbling in Music Production, Game Development, Dog Boarding and Shelf Stacking, I have decided to retrain as a Web Developer and I'm excited to dive back into the tech sector armed with a wide breadth of knowledge and skills added to my previous experience. That needs a rewrite but I'm tired.
